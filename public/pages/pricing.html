Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Free plan actually free? ▼
Yes — permanently. No credit card, no trial period that ends, no catch. You get one complete AI career plan per month with the full roadmap, skills, salary projections, and resources. Free forever.
What makes Premium worth it over Pro? ▼
Premium is for students who want the complete package. The 1-on-1 advisor session alone (30 minutes/month with a human career advisor) is worth more than the subscription price. Add scholarship matching, university shortlisting, parent reports, unlimited career comparisons, and a monthly plan refresh — Premium is genuinely a different product, not just more of the same.
Can I cancel anytime? ▼
Absolutely. Cancel from your account settings at any time — no lock-in, no cancellation fees. If you cancel, you keep access until the end of your billing period. Your saved plans are retained for 90 days after cancellation.
How does the scholarship program work? ▼
Fill in the scholarship application (takes 5 minutes). We review applications at the start of each month and notify recipients by the 5th. Full scholarships are 3 months of Premium. 50% discounts run for 6 months. There's no complicated essay — we just want to understand your situation and your goals.
Who is the 1-on-1 advisor session with? ▼
Sessions are with qualified career counselors who specialize in the UAE and MENA job market. They review your AI-generated plan, give personalized feedback, answer specific questions about your career path, and can help with CV advice, interview prep, and university applications.
Is my data private? ▼
Yes. Your profile information is used only to generate your career plan. We never sell your data or share it with advertisers. Free plan data is not stored after your session. Pro and Premium plan data is securely stored and only accessible to you.
What age is FutureLens for? ▼
FutureLens is built for students aged 13–25, though anyone at a career crossroads can use it. It's particularly powerful for high school students choosing a path, university students picking a major, and young graduates figuring out their next move.
